Taylor, AB Guide to Music Theory part 1 (click) part 2 (click)

here's the introductory book i talked about!
the first book, Part 1, starts off pretty basic, and Part 2 actually goes pretty advanced. for context, i used Part 1 in my intro singing lessons, and i used Part 2 studying composition at tech. after ch. 18 it gets into things specific to part-writing for strings, brasses and reeds, historically-informed performance and post-tonal harmony, which aren't necessarily relevant to you, so just read what's relevant or interesting (although you'd probably find some of that stuff cool).

Aldwell & Schachter, Harmony & Voice Leading (click)

this would follow on from part 2 of the Taylor; while the title suggests the book is about a specific technique called 'voice leading' (arranging a melody with chords underneath), it actually covers most advanced functional harmony. you won't need this for a long time.

Barry Harris, Harmonic Method for Guitar (click)

useful methods for conceptualizing guitar playing, within a jazz framework; primarily forming chords with drop voicings and chord substitutions. should be useful either immediately or after Taylor 1. Barry Harris taught John Coltrane and other well known jazz musicians.

Cook, Guide to Musical Analysis (click)

for analyzing scores, if you want to do that. this isn't necessarily how modern musicology is done but it is traditionally taught.

How to Play from a Real Book (click)

simple book that teaches you how to read a Real Book (scores for jazz standards).
